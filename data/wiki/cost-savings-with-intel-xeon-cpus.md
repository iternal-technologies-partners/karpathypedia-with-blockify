Deploying artificial intelligence workloads on existing Intel Xeon CPU infrastructure offers organizations a compelling alternative to investing in dedicated GPU-based servers, delivering substantial reductions in both upfront capital expenditure and long-term total cost of ownership. By leveraging hardware already in place, companies can avoid approximately $40,000 per server in initial procurement costs while achieving comparable performance for moderate-throughput AI inference tasks.

## Capital Expenditure Reduction

One of the most significant financial advantages of utilizing Intel Xeon CPUs for AI inference is the elimination of large upfront hardware purchases. Specifically, deploying AI on existing Intel Xeon 6767P hardware allows organizations to avoid approximately $40,000 per server in capital expenditure that would otherwise be required to procure new H100 GPU-based servers. This cost avoidance is particularly impactful for organizations already operating Xeon infrastructure, as the transition to AI workloads requires no additional server acquisition investment.

For organizations exploring AI-enabled business functions such as those described in [AutoReports Procurement & Legal Use Cases](/wiki/autoreports-procurement-legal-use-cases), this reduction in upfront spending can make AI adoption significantly more accessible without requiring new budget allocations for specialized accelerator hardware.

## Three-Year Total Cost of Ownership

Beyond immediate capital savings, the long-term financial profile of Xeon-based AI deployment is notably favorable. The projected three-year Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for a Xeon server running AI workloads is $4,221, compared to $55,942 for a new GPU-based server over the same period. This represents an approximately 92% reduction in TCO, a figure that reflects not only the avoidance of initial hardware costs but also the ongoing operational economics of each platform.

This TCO advantage makes the Xeon approach especially attractive for organizations planning multi-year AI deployments where cumulative infrastructure costs are a primary consideration in technology decision-making.

## Cost-Effective Scalability for Low-to-Moderate Throughput

The economic benefits of Intel Xeon-based AI infrastructure are particularly pronounced for workloads that fall within low-to-moderate throughput requirements. For organizations processing fewer than 4.35 billion tokens per year, the Xeon platform delivers superior cost-per-token performance at $0.32 per million tokens, compared to $0.65 per million tokens for a new GPU server when factoring in the initial purchase price.

This cost-per-token advantage means that for a broad range of enterprise AI use cases — including document processing, legal review, procurement analysis, and other text-based inference tasks — Xeon-based deployments provide a more economical path than GPU-based alternatives. Organizations whose workloads do not require the highest possible inference throughput can therefore achieve better unit economics by remaining on existing CPU infrastructure.

## Summary of Key Financial Metrics

The financial case for Intel Xeon-based AI deployment can be summarized across three primary dimensions:

- **Capital Expenditure Avoidance:** Approximately $40,000 per server saved by using existing Intel Xeon 6767P hardware instead of purchasing new H100 GPU servers.
- **Three-Year TCO:** $4,221 per Xeon server versus $55,942 for a new GPU server, representing a 92% TCO reduction.
- **Cost per Token:** $0.32 per million tokens on Xeon versus $0.65 per million tokens on a new GPU server, for workloads below 4.35 billion tokens per year.

Together, these metrics present a strong financial rationale for organizations evaluating AI infrastructure investments, particularly those already operating Intel Xeon environments and seeking to extend the productive life of existing hardware assets.
