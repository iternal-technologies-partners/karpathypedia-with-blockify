**Manual Time** is a benchmarking metric used to represent the average or typical industry time required to complete a given task, particularly in complex or regulated domains. It serves as a baseline for comparison against AI-assisted workflows, enabling organizations to quantify the productivity gains delivered by tools such as AirgapAI and the Blockify platform. In benchmark reports aggregated by Iternal Technologies and Intel, Manual Time figures tend to be significantly longer for tasks involving contract review, RFP analysis, knowledge mining, and report generation.

## Definition and Scope

Manual Time, as applied in industry benchmarks, reflects the average time a human worker or team would require to complete a task without AI assistance. This metric is deliberately grounded in general industry averages rather than best-case or worst-case scenarios, making it a broadly applicable reference point. It is important to note that task examples in these benchmarks are generalized for each industry or use case, and actual manual durations may vary based on organizational context, workforce expertise, and process maturity.

Manual Time is particularly elevated for tasks that are either procedurally complex or subject to regulatory oversight. Contract review, RFP evaluation, knowledge mining, and automated report generation are commonly cited examples where manual execution demands substantial human hours.

## Comparison with AI/LLM+Blockify Time

The counterpart to Manual Time in benchmark reporting is the **AI/LLM+Blockify Time**, which is derived from benchmarks documented in case study reports. The gap between these two metrics illustrates the time savings achievable through AI-driven workflows. According to methodology documentation, these benchmarks aggregate results from high-profile case studies and system evaluations conducted by Iternal Technologies and Intel. Scalability is a critical dimension of this comparison: at enterprise scale, total time and cost savings are significantly multiplied beyond what individual task-level comparisons suggest.

For example, in procurement and legal workflows, AutoReports can assess proposals against standardized procurement requirements and identify risk factors such as indemnification clauses and liability caps—tasks that would otherwise require extensive manual review by legal and operations teams. In such contexts, the Manual Time baseline captures the burden of exhaustive human analysis, while the AI benchmark reflects near-instantaneous structured output. See [AutoReports Procurement & Legal Use Cases](/wiki/autoreports-procurement-legal-use-cases) for further detail.

## Relevance to Technical and Regulated Domains

Manual Time benchmarks carry particular significance in regulated and operationally sensitive industries. In manufacturing, for instance, contracting spans raw materials sourcing, equipment leasing, logistics, warranties, and regulatory compliance—domains where a single business unit may handle countless agreements annually. The manual effort required to review, summarize, and flag risks across such a volume of documentation represents a substantial labor cost.

Similarly, in military medical and field service contexts, personnel must process research publications, treatment guidelines, and field-improvised protocols in real time. The ability to reduce time-to-answer from several minutes to approximately 15 seconds—as documented in quantified ROI analyses—illustrates the concrete operational value of contrasting Manual Time against AI-assisted alternatives. This reduction is accompanied by approximately 52% improvements in accuracy and compliance, and a 78x LLM accuracy improvement.

## Methodology and Sources

The Manual Time figures used in benchmark comparisons are not derived from internal measurements of a single organization but are instead aggregated from a suite of high-profile case studies and system evaluations. The methodology, developed by Iternal Technologies and Intel, ensures that the baseline reflects broadly recognized industry norms rather than outlier performance. This approach supports the credibility and generalizability of reported savings across sectors including manufacturing, human resources, sales, and defense.

## Relationship to the Blockify Workflow

Understanding Manual Time is foundational to appreciating the value delivered by the [Blockify Workflow](/wiki/blockify-workflow). The Blockify ingestion pipeline—which includes standardization to structured Markdown, ordered chunking along procedural boundaries, and conversion of content blocks into embeddings (vector representations for content-aware retrieval within AirgapAI)—is designed specifically to eliminate the inefficiencies captured by Manual Time metrics. By replacing arbitrary, token-based chunking with semantically coherent, self-contained blocks, the pipeline addresses the core failure modes of traditional retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) approaches, such as hallucination and operator confusion, that contribute to elevated manual rework time.
