AutoReports, a registered trademark of Iternal Technologies, offers a suite of document intelligence capabilities applicable to procurement and legal workflows. By digitizing and processing large volumes of structured and unstructured content, AutoReports enables teams to extract actionable insights from materials that once remained inaccessible within PDFs and Word documents. Its configurable query architecture and hardware compatibility make it a versatile tool for organizations managing complex contractual and vendor-related documentation.

## Data Digitization and Blockification

A foundational capability underlying AutoReports' procurement and legal applications is the process of data digitization and "Blockification." Massive volumes of data are digitized and Blockified, making it possible to extract, collate, and act upon crucial insights that once stayed buried in PDFs or Word documents. This transformation of raw, legacy content into structured, queryable units is what enables downstream procurement and legal use cases to function at scale. Related automation capabilities — such as those used to process terabytes of legacy policy documents and claims files — demonstrate how Blockification can significantly improve data accuracy and speed up curation workflows.

## Contract Data Verification

One of the more critical applications of AutoReports in legal and procurement contexts is contract data verification. Contract language is chunked and verified, ensuring procurement teams can trust that automatically extracted data — such as payment schedules — is accurate before final sign-off. This verification layer is particularly significant for organizations managing high volumes of agreements, where manual review introduces risk and delays. The chunking methodology aligns with the broader Blockification approach, applying structured segmentation to contractual text to facilitate reliable extraction.

## RFP and RFI Processing

AutoReports also addresses the demands of vendor evaluation by handling Request for Proposal (RFP) and Request for Information (RFI) documentation. AutoReports extracts must-have specifications and compliance terms from vendor proposals, facilitating procurement decisions. This capability reduces the manual effort required to parse lengthy vendor submissions and ensures that critical requirements and compliance criteria are surfaced efficiently for procurement teams.

## Equipment Lease Analysis

For organizations managing physical assets, AutoReports provides dedicated support for equipment lease analysis. AutoReports summarizes lease timelines, extension options, and SLA details, streamlining equipment lease management. By consolidating key contractual elements into accessible summaries, procurement and operations teams can make more informed decisions regarding asset renewals and service-level obligations without manually reviewing each agreement in full.

## Query Configuration and IdeaBlock Integration

The analytical capabilities of AutoReports are made available through a fully configurable query system. Queries in AutoReports are fully configurable and can be deployed as LLM prompts via the Critical Question prompt field contained within each IdeaBlock. This architecture allows procurement and legal teams to tailor document interrogation to their specific needs, whether identifying compliance clauses, extracting financial terms, or surfacing renewal deadlines. The flexibility of this system supports a wide range of document types encountered in procurement and legal environments. For more on how responses are generated from these queries, see [Retrieval and Response Generation](/wiki/retrieval-and-response-generation).

## Hardware Compatibility

AutoReports is designed to operate across a range of hardware configurations. It supports Intel Xeon CPUs and is compatible with both NVIDIA and AMD GPUs. This breadth of hardware support allows organizations to deploy AutoReports within existing infrastructure without requiring specialized or proprietary equipment. For organizations evaluating the financial implications of CPU-based deployments, see [Cost Savings with Intel Xeon CPUs](/wiki/cost-savings-with-intel-xeon-cpus).
