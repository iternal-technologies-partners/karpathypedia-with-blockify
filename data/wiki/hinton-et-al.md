### What is knowledge distillation and how is it used to create smaller, efficient models?

Knowledge distillation is a model compression technique where a smaller 'student' model is trained to mimic the behavior of a larger 'teacher' model, transferring the teacher's learned knowledge into a more compact form. Introduced by Hinton et al. (2015), the key insight is that the teacher's soft probability distributions over output classes (softmax outputs at a raised temperature) contain richer information than hard labels alone — the relative probabilities reveal inter-class similarities the teacher has learned. The student is trained with a combined loss: a distillation loss (KL divergence between student and teacher soft distributions) and a standard cross-entropy loss on the true labels. In the LLM era, distillation has become crucial for deploying capable models efficiently: techniques include training smaller models on outputs generated by larger models (as in Alpaca, which used GPT-3.5 outputs to train a 7B model), progressive distillation for diffusion models, and task-specific distillation where a large general model teaches a smaller specialist. Distillation enables running near-frontier-quality models on edge devices, reducing latency and inference cost by orders of magnitude.
