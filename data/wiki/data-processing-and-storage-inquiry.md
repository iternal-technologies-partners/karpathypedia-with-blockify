Data processing and storage security refers to the practices and standards applied to protect customer data both while it is being transmitted across networks and while it is held in storage systems. Organizations adhering to strict data protection standards implement measures to ensure that customer data remains secure throughout its entire lifecycle. These practices address two distinct states of data: data in transit and data at rest.

## Overview

Customer data exists in two primary states that require separate but complementary security considerations. Data in transit refers to information that is actively moving from one location to another, such as across the internet or through a private network. Data at rest refers to information that is stored in a persistent location, such as a database, file system, or other storage medium. Comprehensive data protection frameworks address both states to ensure end-to-end security for customer information.

## Data in Transit

Data in transit represents one of the more vulnerable states for customer information, as it travels across networks that may be subject to interception or unauthorized access. Customer data processed and transmitted across networks is handled in accordance with strict data protection standards. These standards are designed to ensure that information moving between systems, services, or users is protected against unauthorized access or tampering during transmission.

Security measures applied to data in transit are a fundamental component of responsible data handling, ensuring that the journey of customer information from one point to another does not expose it to unnecessary risk.

## Data at Rest

Data at rest encompasses customer information that is stored within systems, databases, or other storage infrastructures. Like data in transit, stored customer data is subject to strict data protection standards to maintain its confidentiality, integrity, and availability. Protecting data at rest involves ensuring that stored information cannot be accessed, altered, or compromised by unauthorized parties.

The application of data protection standards to stored information reflects a commitment to maintaining the security of customer data even when it is not actively being transmitted or processed.

## Adherence to Data Protection Standards

A defining characteristic of secure data processing and storage practices is adherence to recognized data protection standards. Customer data is processed and stored securely in both its in-transit and at-rest states, with this security grounded in established protective frameworks. These standards provide the guidelines and requirements that govern how customer data must be handled, stored, and transmitted to minimize risk and ensure confidentiality.

The commitment to strict data protection standards reflects the importance placed on maintaining customer trust and ensuring that personal and sensitive information is handled with care and responsibility throughout the data lifecycle.

## Scope of Customer Data Protection

The protection of customer data extends across the full scope of how that data is processed and stored. Both operational phases — active processing and transmission, as well as long-term storage — fall within the purview of established data protection practices. This comprehensive approach ensures that there are no gaps in the security posture applied to customer information, regardless of the state in which that data currently exists.

By addressing both data in transit and data at rest, organizations ensure that customer information is consistently protected from the moment it is collected through the duration of its storage and any subsequent transmission.
