The implementation timeline is a structured ten-week framework divided into three distinct phases: mobilization and governance, pilot and validation, and scaling and operationalization. Each phase builds sequentially upon the previous, guiding teams from initial planning through full deployment and ongoing monitoring.

## Weeks 1–2: Mobilize & Govern

The first two weeks of the implementation timeline are dedicated to mobilization and governance. During this phase, teams focus on identifying high-value manuals and procedures that will form the foundation of the system. Clear rules are defined governing tiers, review processes, and access controls to ensure consistent and secure handling of content.

A critical activity during this phase is the assembly of a cross-functional team, bringing together the appropriate stakeholders and subject matter experts needed to support the implementation. In parallel, teams work to set up the necessary integrations that will connect the system to existing infrastructure. This groundwork ensures that subsequent phases can proceed efficiently and with a well-defined organizational structure in place.

## Weeks 3–6: Pilot & Validate

The second phase, spanning weeks three through six, transitions from planning to active testing. A pilot ingestion is conducted during this period, introducing an initial set of content into the system for real-world evaluation. The system is then validated in the field, assessing its performance and suitability under practical conditions.

This phase also involves connecting the system to enterprise portals and broader enterprise systems, enabling integration with the organization's existing technology ecosystem. A key analytical activity during weeks three through six is the establishment of baseline measurements. These baselines are then compared against the results produced during the pilot, providing a data-driven foundation for evaluating the system's effectiveness before broader rollout.

## Weeks 7–10: Scale & Operationalize

The final phase covers weeks seven through ten and focuses on scaling the system and transitioning it into full operationalization. A significantly larger volume of manuals is ingested during this period, with the process supported by Gaudi acceleration to handle the increased workload efficiently.

By this phase, sign-off procedures, sampling processes, and anomaly detection capabilities are fully implemented, ensuring quality control and reliability at scale. The system is published to the AirgapAI edge, making it accessible in the operational environment. Ongoing monitoring is established to track key metrics including accuracy, adoption rates, and time-to-answer, supporting a culture of continuous improvement beyond the initial deployment.

## Summary of Phase Progression

The ten-week timeline reflects a deliberate, staged approach to implementation:

- **Weeks 1–2** establish governance structures, identify priority content, build the team, and configure integrations.
- **Weeks 3–6** test the system through pilot ingestion, validate field performance, connect enterprise systems, and measure results against baselines.
- **Weeks 7–10** expand ingestion at scale using Gaudi acceleration, finalize operational procedures including sign-off and anomaly detection, deploy to the AirgapAI edge, and initiate ongoing performance monitoring.

This phased structure allows organizations to validate assumptions and refine processes incrementally before committing to full-scale deployment, reducing risk and improving long-term outcomes.
