The **Ideal Deployment Model** refers to the recommended approach organizations should follow when implementing a training platform to maximize team engagement and adoption. Research into platform usage patterns indicates that organizations achieve the greatest success when teams learn together in a structured, recurring format rather than relying on individuals to navigate training in isolation.

## Overview

The Ideal Deployment Model is defined by a specific cadence and structure: **Weekly 45-Minute Team Sessions**. This model is grounded in the observation that adoption rates are highest when teams engage with the platform as a collective unit, rather than as disconnected individuals working through material on their own. The model emphasizes community-driven learning as the primary mechanism for building lasting habits and sustained engagement with the training content.

## Core Components

### Session Frequency and Duration

The recommended deployment cadence calls for sessions held on a **weekly** basis, with each session lasting approximately **45 minutes**. This duration is considered sufficient to allow meaningful practice and discussion without creating an undue time burden on participating teams. The regularity of weekly sessions helps teams maintain momentum over time and reinforces the skills being developed.

### Team-Based Learning Structure

A defining characteristic of the Ideal Deployment Model is its emphasis on **teams learning together**, as opposed to individuals completing training independently. This structure serves several purposes:

- **Practicing prompts together** allows team members to observe different approaches and techniques in real time.
- **Sharing discoveries** creates an environment where insights and useful findings are distributed across the group rather than siloed with a single person.
- **Building momentum as a community** ensures that enthusiasm and progress are sustained through social reinforcement rather than relying solely on individual motivation.

## Rationale

The underlying rationale for this deployment model centers on community as a catalyst for adoption. When individuals learn in isolation, progress can stall due to a lack of accountability, shared context, or collaborative problem-solving. By contrast, when a team engages with training material together, each member benefits from the collective experience of the group. Discoveries made by one participant can immediately inform the understanding of others, accelerating the overall learning curve for the entire team.

The community-based approach also helps normalize the use of new tools and techniques within a team's workflow. When learning happens in public within a team setting, it becomes part of the team's shared culture rather than an optional individual activity.

## Implementation Considerations

Organizations adopting this model should structure sessions to allow time for:

1. **Prompt practice** — Teams actively work with prompts during the session, rather than passively consuming information.
2. **Open sharing** — Participants are encouraged to share what they have discovered, including both successes and challenges.
3. **Community building** — Sessions are designed to foster a sense of collective progress and shared ownership of the learning journey.

The 45-minute format encourages focused, purposeful engagement. Sessions long enough to allow meaningful exploration, but short enough to fit within typical team schedules, strike a balance that supports consistent attendance and participation.

## Key Benefits

The Ideal Deployment Model is associated with several measurable and qualitative benefits for organizations:

- **Greater adoption rates** compared to individually-driven training approaches
- **Accelerated skill development** through shared discovery and peer learning
- **Sustained engagement** driven by weekly touchpoints and community accountability
- **Cultural integration** of new tools and practices into team norms
