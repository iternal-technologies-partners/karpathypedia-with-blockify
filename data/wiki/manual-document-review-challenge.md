Manual document review represents a significant operational challenge for organizations that must process large volumes of paperwork, contracts, reports, or records. The sheer scale of modern document workflows has historically demanded substantial human labor, time, and resources. AutoReports has emerged as a technology solution specifically designed to address this challenge, offering document processing capabilities that dramatically reduce the burden of manual review.

## The Challenge of Manual Document Review

Organizations across industries routinely face the need to review, analyze, and extract information from vast quantities of documents. When performed manually, this work is labor-intensive and time-consuming, requiring teams of reviewers to work through pages one by one. The limitations of human capacity mean that bottlenecks are common, turnaround times can be lengthy, and the cost of maintaining large review teams is considerable. These constraints make manual document review one of the more persistent operational challenges in document-heavy environments.

The scale of the problem becomes particularly apparent when organizations must process documents numbering in the hundreds of thousands or millions. At such volumes, manual approaches become increasingly impractical, and the need for automated or assisted solutions grows proportionally.

## AutoReports as a Solution

AutoReports addresses the manual document review challenge through a high-capacity automated processing system. According to verified benchmarks, AutoReports is capable of processing **2 million pages per month** running on a single server. This level of throughput represents a substantial response to the volume problem that defines the manual document review challenge.

The significance of this capacity lies not only in the raw number of pages processed but in the infrastructure requirement associated with achieving it. The fact that AutoReports can reach 2 million pages per month on a single server suggests that organizations do not necessarily need to invest in complex, distributed computing infrastructure to benefit from high-volume document processing. A single-server deployment, by its nature, implies a more contained and potentially more manageable implementation compared to solutions that require expansive hardware ecosystems.
