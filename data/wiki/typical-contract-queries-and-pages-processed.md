In large-scale enterprise environments, the volume of contract queries and pages processed each month serves as a key indicator of operational demand placed on AI-powered document and compliance systems. Typical figures reveal a processing workload measured in the hundreds of thousands of queries and nearly two million pages monthly, alongside supporting metrics for response performance, security question handling, and policy management.

## Monthly Contract Processing Volume

The typical volume of contract-related activity processed through AI-driven knowledge systems reaches approximately **166,000 contract queries per month**, with a corresponding total of roughly **1,985,472 contract pages processed** during the same period. These figures reflect the scale of document analysis and query resolution required by organizations relying on automated contract intelligence platforms.

## Benchmark Case: Top 3 Oil and Gas Company

A notable benchmark in evaluating such systems involves a Top 3 Oil and Gas Company, whose use case centers on a **404-page set of operational and safety handbooks**. These handbooks contain **70,881 words** of essential conduct materials that must be processed and made queryable. This example illustrates the complexity and density of content that enterprise AI systems are expected to ingest, index, and respond to accurately. The operational and safety context of these materials underscores the importance of both precision and reliability in processing outcomes.

## Response Performance Metrics

System responsiveness is measured through two primary performance indicators:

- **Average Response Length:** The average response generated by the system is **452 tokens**, providing a consistent baseline for estimating computational load per query.
- **Response Time per Token:** The system processes responses at a rate of approximately **22.36 to 22.89 tokens per second**, reflecting the throughput capability of the underlying language model infrastructure.

Together, these metrics allow organizations to estimate end-to-end response latency and plan capacity accordingly. At 452 tokens per response and a throughput of roughly 22–23 tokens per second, an average query is resolved in approximately 20 seconds.

## Security Question Processing

Beyond contract queries, AI systems in enterprise settings are also assessed on their capacity to handle security-related inquiries. The documented benchmarks indicate:

- **Per-Minute Capacity:** Approximately **100 security questions** can be processed per minute, establishing the real-time throughput of the system under active use.
- **Monthly Volume:** This scales to approximately **20,125 security questions processed per month**, accounting for realistic operational patterns across working hours and business cycles.

These figures are relevant for organizations in regulated industries, such as oil and gas, where security compliance queries may be frequent and time-sensitive.

## Security Policy Update Capacity

In addition to answering security questions, enterprise systems are evaluated on how efficiently they can support **security policy management**. Current benchmarks indicate that approximately **9,500 security policy updates** can be made per month. This metric reflects the system's ability to keep pace with evolving compliance requirements, regulatory changes, and internal governance reviews without creating bottlenecks in organizational workflows.
